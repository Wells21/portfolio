Customer Booking Prediction

Tools used -

Project scenario -


Customers are more empowered than ever because they have access to a wealth of information at their fingertips. This is one of the reasons the buying cycle is very different to what it used to be. Today, if you’re hoping that a customer purchases your flights or holidays as they come into the airport, you’ve already lost! Being reactive in this situation is not ideal; airlines must be proactive in order to acquire customers before they embark on their holiday.


This is possible with the use of data and predictive models. The most important factor with a predictive model is the quality of the data you use to train the machine learning algorithms. For this task, we must manipulate and prepare the provided customer booking data so that we can build a high-quality predictive model.


Therefore in this project, we would be buillding a predictive model to understand factors that influence buying behaviour

- Evaluation Metric


    The Evaluation metric for a machine learning project should be based on the problem, so therefore understanding the problem and the soluton to the problem is important.


We have 4 different evaluation metric:

1. Accuracy
2. Recall

3. Precision

4. F1


Lets explain the 4 different metrics


1. Accuracy: It's a metric that measures how often a machine learning model correctly predicts the outcome.


2. Recall: It's a metric that measures how often a machine learning model correctly identifies positive instances (true positives) from all the actual positive samples in the dataset.


3. Precision: It's a metric that measures how often a machine learning model correctly predicts the positive class.


4. F1 score is a measure of the harmonic mean of precision and recall.

In our problem, what do you think our evaluation metric would be?


The answer to that question would be an answer to this question, "what is the cost of predicting a patience has no cancer disease when the patient actually has cancer disease?"


Same goes to our problem, "what is the cost of predicting a client won't book when the client actually booked?"


Therefore our evaluation metric chosen would be Recall. Recall tells us the percentage of correctly identifying the postive class (i.e 1, which is True, the client booked).
