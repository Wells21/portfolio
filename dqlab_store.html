SQL Sales Analysis

DQLab Store

Tool used -

Project scenario -


A retail transaction dataset over four years. This project analyzes the sales performance of the DQLab Store using MySQL.

Data Description -


The data consists of historical sales data from 2009 to 2012. This data contain 3 product category which are office supplies, technology, and furniture. Each category has several sub-categories. The company also runs promotional in the form of a discount


Data Source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/dhawyfarrasputra/sales-performance-report-dqlab-store?select=clean_data.csv

Thought Process -


- Understanding the problem


    First of all, before diving into a project, you ask yourself, what is the project all about? What do you aim to achieve at the end of the project? Are you providing recommendations?


Understanding a problem is important for an accurate and efective analysis.


In this project, the problem aims to analyze the trends of sales in the DQLab Store.


- Understanding the DATA


    There are cases whereby the understanding of the project is attained, and you know what the problem is all about, but if there arent significant data that can help solve the problem, there is no way to attain to a solution. The solution to a problem lies in the data, and so the right thing to do is to understand the data first before performing the analysis.


This is a snapshot of part of the dataset variables.


The data, contains weather variables, (whethersit , etc), and seasonal variables, that helps in performing the time series analysis.


- Data Cleaning


    For an accurate analysis, it is important to clean the data, many data comes in messy forms, can be incossistent accross multiple source, can contain many missing values, which if performed without cleaning can lead to noise in the time series model, or can lead to biasness in decision-making.


For this data, it contains no missing values, incorrect format on the date variable, expresses as a string object, instead of as a datetime object. Therefore, I performed a correction of format acrross the variable and the remaining variables.


- Data Analysis


1.  Top 5 customers producing Highest Revenues

2.  What is the Product Category producing Highest Revenues

3.  Product Category cantaining more Quantity Ordered

4.  Top 5 Sub-Product Categories producing Highest Revenues

5.  Top 5 Sub-Product Categories containing more Quanity Ordered

6.  On what Month did the highest producing revenue occured?

7.  How many orders were completed ?

8.  How many orders were returned?

9.  How many orders were cancelled?

Using MySQL to answer the business questions-


1.  Top 5 customers producing Highest Revenues

The Query Results shows that Deborah Brumfield generates more revenues in the company than any other customers.

2.  What is the Product Category producing Highest Revenues

The Query Results shows that the product category Technology generates more revenues in the company.

3.  Product Category containing more Quantity Ordered

The Query Results shows that the product category Office Supplies has more customers orders. It tells us that the product that contains office supplies are needed more by customers.

4.  Top 5 Sub-Product Categories producing Highest Revenues

5.  Top 5 Sub-Product Categories containing more Quanity Ordered

6.  On what Month did the highest producing revenue occured?

The Query Results shows the month in which the most revenue was generated, which is the month of January (begining of the year), and then December which is the Christmas Month. This shows that revenues were maximum at the begining of the year, and then at the end of the year.

7.  How many orders were completed ?

Out of 5499 orders made, 4924 were completed.

8.  How many orders were returned?

Out of 5499 orders made, 572 were returned. Meaning that after the item was bought and delivered, it was then returned , i.e, there were'nt a succesful order, due to some issues.

Further investigation should be done to those products in which the order was returned.

9.  How many orders were cancelled?

Out of 5499 orders made, 3 were cancelled. Meaning that in the process of buying the item, the operation was cancelled, due to unforeseen circumstances.
